Ethical Considerations

 

A major component of successful research is ethical considerations.

There can be serious consequences if ethical considerations are ignored.

As a fundamental requirement for doing scientific research, scientists must always preserve ethical issues in the field.

 

1. Possession

According to Catherine Cote (2021), A person's ownership of their confidential information is the first rule of data ethics. 

Collecting someone's personal information without their consent is illegal and unethical, just as it is deemed theft to take something that doesn't belong to you.

 

2. Transparency 

According to Catherine Cote (2021), Data subjects have a right to know how you intend to gather, store, and use their confidential information in addition to owning it. 

Be transparent when collecting statistics.

 

3. Personal privacy

According to Catherine Cote (2021), A further ethical obligation associated with data handling is protecting the private of data subjects.

Customers may not want their personally identifiable information (PII) made public even if they grant your business permission to gather, store, and analyze it.

 

This is sensitive information that I cannot leak about people when doing this study:

 

  • Entire name
  • Date of birth 
  • Street location 
  • Phone number
  • Social Security number 
  • Details from credit cards 
  • Bank account details 
  • Passport number

 

 

4. Objective

According to Catherine Cote (2021), Intentions count when talking about ethics in any area.

Ask yourself why you need the data, what you'll gain from it, and what adjustments you'll be able to make once it's been analyzed before you start gathering it. It is unethical to collect your subjects' data if your aim is to harm them, make money off their vulnerabilities, or pursue any other nefarious objective.

 

According to Catherine Cote (2021) Even if your goals are good—for example, gathering information to better understand women's healthcare experiences so you can develop an app to fill a critical need—you should still consider your motivations before collecting any data.

 

According to Catherine Cote (2021) Exist any data points that don't relate to the current issue? Is it essential, for instance, to inquire about the participants' mental health issues? It is unethical to collect this data when it is not required because it may be sensitive. In order to take the least amount of information from your subjects while still having an impact, try to gather the bare minimum amount of data.
