Proposed Title

Title:

Privacy and security in Big Data Environments a case study of the banking and institutional sector in South Africa.

 

 

Research Details

Problem Statement:

Privacy and Security are a prolonged risk to big data security facilities in the banking sector of South African businesses

Purpose Statement:

I will research methods that will Increase the Privacy and Security of Big Data in the financial and institutional sector, provide possible prevention methods for database breaches and safe data storage, and raise awareness.

 

I will build a model: 

Research Questions:
  1. Will the financial and institutional sector sell its data storage what ethics involve the privacy of the users that are associated with this sector?
  2. How can an employee’s details be at risk in a bank and university?
  3. How could a single students or employee’s data theft lead to the university or bank’s Big Data loss?
  4. How to raise awareness about cybercrimes?

 

Hypothesis/Objectives:

1. Spread awareness about data theft in the banking and institutional sector.

2. Develop an informational model to provide awareness of privacy and security in big data in the banking sector.

 

Research Rationale

It is necessary to do this research because the financial record in the financial sector is important and should be heavily encrypted. If data stored is lost and not encrypted this could lead to lawsuits against a company holding this data. My research is to bring awareness of safer ways to store large data that deal with financial records. It is essential for a reader to understand that this is regards database breaches and profile breaches. After reading my research the reader will have a better understanding of ways to increase the privacy and security of their big data in the financial sector.

Ethical Considerations & Limitations

Ethics:

1. Possession

According to Catherine Cote (2021), person's ownership of their confidential information is the first rule of data ethics. 

 

My thought is collecting someone's personal information without their consent is illegal and unethical, just as it is deemed theft to take something that doesn't belong to you.

 

2.Transparency 

According to Catherine Cote (2021), Data subjects have a right to know how you intend to gather, store, and use their confidential information in addition to owning it. 

 

My advice is be transparent when collecting statistics.

 

3. Personal privacy

According to Catherine Cote (2021), A further ethical obligation associated with data handling is protecting the private of data subjects.

 My thought is customers may not want their personally identifiable information (PII) made public even if they grant your business permission to gather, store, and analyze it.

 

PII refers to any data associated with a specific person's identify. PII includes, for instance:

 

  • Entire name
  • Date of birth 
  • Street location 
  • Phone number
  • Social Security number 
  • Details from credit cards 
  • Bank account details 
  • Passport number

 

 

4. Objective

According to Catherine Cote (2021), Intentions count when talking about ethics in any area.

 

Ask yourself why you need the data, what you'll gain from it, and what adjustments you'll be able to make once it's been analyzed before you start gathering it. 

 

I think it is unethical to collect your subjects' data if you aim to harm them, make money off of their vulnerabilities, or pursue any other nefarious objective.

 

According to Catherine Cote (2021) Even if your goals are good—for example, gathering information to better understand women's healthcare experiences so you can develop an app to fill a critical need—you should still consider your motivations before collecting any data.

 

According to Catherine Cote (2021) Exist any data points that don't relate to the current issue? Is it essential, for instance, to inquire about the participants' mental health issues? It is unethical to collect this data when it is not required because it may be sensitive. In order to take the least amount of information from your subjects while still having an impact, try to gather the bare minimum amount of data.

 

 

Limitations:

My limitation to this study is that I do not have the funds to continue testing cybersecurity models in large-scale banks.

My limitation is that I cannot test different banking systems as the banks may see me as a hacker and possibly open a lawsuit. 

My limitation is that I have not gone to a bank itself to test their system because again they could open a lawsuit against me for trying to breach their system.

My limitation is that I cannot open the banks’ database as it is private.

My limitation is that I cannot view any bank analytics because I am not signed by contract with the bank.

My limitation is that if I see any of the banks’ clients that will be a privacy breach because that client signed the agreement that they and the bank will see their data. 

The bank without an agreement before viewing their data could risk the cyber security personnel selling the information to another bank.

My limitation is that the bank cannot trust me unless I can show them I can breach the system, But by breaching the system the bank can file a lawsuit.
